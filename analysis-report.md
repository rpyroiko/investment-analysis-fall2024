## 6. Comparative Insights
- **Growth:** Microsoft’s revenue grew faster YoY and has a higher operating margin, indicating stronger profitability.
- **Valuation:** Apple trades at a slightly lower forward P/E, but both are premium‑priced relative to market averages.
- **Dividend:** Microsoft offers a higher dividend yield, appealing for income‑focused investors.
- **Risk:** Apple’s higher beta suggests more price volatility; Microsoft’s lower beta reflects a steadier stock.
- **Catalysts:** AI integration (Apple Intelligence, Microsoft Copilot) and cloud/gaming expansions are key growth drivers for both companies.

## 7. Investment Thesis
- **Apple:** Attractive for investors seeking brand strength, ecosystem lock‑in, and modest dividend. Risks include competitive pressure in China and regulatory fines.
- **Microsoft:** Strong cash flow, diversified cloud and gaming businesses, higher dividend, and lower volatility make it a robust core holding.

**Conclusion:** For a balanced portfolio, a weighted allocation favoring Microsoft (≈60%) with Apple (≈40%) leverages Microsoft’s higher growth and dividend while retaining exposure to Apple’s consumer ecosystem and AI potential.
